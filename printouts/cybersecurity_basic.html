Concepts You Need to Know:

Let’s go over the basic concepts of cybersecurity. Understanding these will help you fill in the blanks correctly!

1. Passwords

Your password is your first line of defense. It’s important to use strong, unique passwords for each account.

A **strong password** should be at least 12 characters long and include a mix of letters (uppercase and lowercase), numbers, and symbols.

Example of a weak password: password123

Example of a strong password: G4$hY8_&lP9

2. Encryption

**Encryption** scrambles data so that only the person with the correct key can read it. It helps keep your information safe from hackers.

For example, when you send a message online, encryption changes it from readable text (like “Hello”) to scrambled code (like 9f1e0a) that only the recipient can decode.

This is especially important when sending sensitive information, like passwords or personal data.

3. Firewalls

A **firewall** is a security system that monitors and controls incoming and outgoing network traffic. It acts like a gatekeeper, blocking unwanted connections and allowing trusted ones.

Firewalls help protect your device from attacks by filtering traffic and preventing unauthorized access.

4. Phishing

**Phishing** is a type of cyberattack where a hacker pretends to be someone you trust to steal your information.

For example, you might get an email that looks like it’s from your bank asking you to “verify your account.” But the email is fake, and clicking the link could give hackers access to your account.

Always check if the sender’s email address looks suspicious, and never click on links or download attachments from unknown sources!

Make sure you understand these concepts before moving to the next page!
